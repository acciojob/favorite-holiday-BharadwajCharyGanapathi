Arunachalam

The Arunachalam Temple, also known as Arunachalesvara Temple, is located at the base of Arunachala hill in Tiruvannamalai, Tamil Nadu, India. It is significant in the Hindu sect of Shaivism and is one of the Pancha Bhuta Sthalas, representing the element of fire (Agni). The temple is renowned for its beautiful Dravidian architecture, featuring towering gopurams (temple gateways) and intricate carvings, making it a respected pilgrimage site. The main deity, Adi Arunasaleswarar, symbolizes the cosmic dance of creation and destruction. Additionally, it is one of the five Shiva temples dedicated to nature's elements, enhancing its spiritual significance.
